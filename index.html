IntelliFly

A one-button nano-drone that autonomously scans indoor spaces and turns them into ready-to-use BIM/CAD files, for architects and engineers who can't trust the building plans they've got.

What we're building

Renovation projects almost always start with building documentation that's outdated, incomplete, or missing entirely, forcing architects and HVAC/installation engineers to re-survey sites by hand. IntelliFly is a palm-sized autonomous drone that flies GPS-denied indoor spaces at the press of one button, using multi-zone time-of-flight sensing (with optional thermal) to map rooms without a pilot. It returns to its charging dock on its own and exports the scan straight into the formats teams already use, like ArchiCAD, cutting a manual survey down to a few minutes of flight time.

Developing and testing autonomous flight

Autonomous indoor flight is being built and validated iteratively: each revision of the obstacle-avoidance and mapping logic is flown and compared against the previous one to track real progress, not just simulated performance.
